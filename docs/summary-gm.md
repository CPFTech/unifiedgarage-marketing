# UnifiedGarage — A modern dealer management system

UnifiedGarage replaces the seven separate tools your team opens every morning with one. Instead of bouncing between an inventory spreadsheet, a desking tool, a separate F&I menu app, the service writer screen, a parts lookup, a CRM, and an accounting system, your whole store works out of one piece of software.

## Why it exists

Most DMS platforms were built in the 90s and added bolt-ons for everything new. The result is a stack that doesn't talk to itself — your team re-keys the same VIN three times, customers wait for paperwork because parts and service can't see each other, and accounting closes a month late because deal posts get stuck.

UnifiedGarage was built from scratch as one platform. Inventory talks to deals, deals talk to F&I, F&I posts to accounting, customer info flows everywhere — without anyone re-keying anything.

## What every role on the floor sees

**Sales floor** — Today's ups, your active deals, follow-up reminders, and a desking screen that lets you quote three payment scenarios side-by-side without exiting to find the customer's trade.

**F&I manager** — Menu selling that the customer actually understands, lender submissions, product attach tracking, e-sign packages that drop into the deal jacket automatically.

**Service advisor** — Write an RO with full intake (fuel level, key tag, mileage in, source), assign it to a tech, get the customer's approval by text before any wrench turns, then push it to billing in one click. The dispatch board shows you what's where.

**Technician** — A mobile-friendly "my work" view with assigned jobs, parts status, inspection capture (with photos), and a clock-in/clock-out per job line so productivity reports actually reflect reality.

**Parts** — Stock lookup, purchase orders with receiving, special orders linked to RO lines so the advisor knows exactly when the part lands.

**Accounting** — A real dealership chart of accounts. Deal posts, RO posts, payments, month-end close, schedules, commissions — and a one-click QuickBooks Online push.

**BDC / lead handling** — Inbound leads from web, walk-in, phone, recall campaigns, and third-party sources land in one inbox. Round-robin assignment, SLA tracking, follow-up reminders, conversion to a deal in two clicks.

**Manager / GM** — One dashboard with the numbers that actually matter: funded gross this period, pipeline value, inventory turn, F&I per copy, tech efficiency, lead conversion, days on lot. Drill from any number into the underlying records.

## What's included out of the box

- **Inventory & recon** — landed-to-front-line tracking with photos, VIN decode, market-based pricing, aging alerts
- **Deals & F&I** — worksheet, multi-scenario desking, F&I menu, e-sign, deal jacket, automatic GL posting
- **Service & parts** — repair orders, dispatch board, multi-point inspections, technician time clock, parts inventory, purchase orders
- **CRM & lead routing** — capture, assign, follow up, convert
- **Accounting** — dealership-shaped chart of accounts, journal entries, month-end, QuickBooks Online integration
- **Trade-in appraisals** — structured form with multiple book values, manager bump-approval, automatic deal jacket attachment
- **Customer portal** — your customers see their vehicles, service history, documents, book appointments and pay ROs from their phone
- **Multi-rooftop** — run three stores or thirty out of one platform; share customers across rooftops, transfer inventory, see group-level reports
- **Reports** — gross per copy, F&I penetration, turn time, inventory aging, technician efficiency, CSI, lead conversion

## What switching looks like

1. **Import your data.** Bring inventory, customers, and open deals from Reynolds, CDK, Dealertrack, or a CSV export. We map fields and flag anything that looks off before go-live.
2. **Train your team.** Each role gets a guided walkthrough built for them. Most users are productive in under an hour. Technicians and parts clerks usually under fifteen minutes.
3. **Go live.** Run both systems in parallel for a week, then cut over. We're on call for the first weekend so nothing drops.

Most dealers desk their first real UnifiedGarage deal within seven days of signup.
